How I Work

I think before I build

The best design work starts with real problem framing — not jumping to wireframes. I spend time understanding the domain, the constraints, and what's actually being asked before I touch a tool. Design should drive decisions, not execute them.

I see the whole before the parts

Enterprise platforms are ecosystems. I approach complex problems by mapping how pieces connect before designing any single one — because a good feature in the wrong system can make things worse. Coherence at scale is the actual design challenge.

I work well in the messy middle

The problems I'm drawn to don't have clean briefs. They involve competing priorities, half-formed requirements, and stakeholders who disagree. I'm comfortable holding ambiguity, aligning across functions, and making directional calls with incomplete information.

I make the people around me better

Whether I'm working alongside other designers or partnering with PMs and engineers, I push for stronger thinking — not just stronger output. I ask the questions that make the work sharper, and I share what I know without making it feel like a lesson.
